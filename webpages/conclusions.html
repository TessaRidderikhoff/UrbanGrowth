  • Cellular automata can be used to simulate the spatial structure of urban land use over time.
  • Fractal structures are observed for the urbanized area. 
  • Fractal dimensions found in the model were in agreement with fractal dimensions in the real world.
  • By varying α the randomness and the formation of clusters in the model can be controlled.
  • Local interactions are mostly sufficient for emergent behaviour. Smaller neighbourhood sizes than initially expected were sufficient.
  • The city size distribution follows a power law distribution.
  • The model can be extended, for example by adding a river.
